Who Is Carsten Höller?

Carsten Höller, a Belgian artist born in Brussels in 1961 with a Ph.D. in agricultural science, is renowned for his diverse approach to art, integrating science, psychology, and architecture. His influential career spans various fields, reflecting in the conceptual depth of his works. Notably, his 2006 "Test Site" installation at Tate Modern featured massive slides, blending thrill with artistic engagement.

Another significant piece is "SOMA," unveiled in 2020 at Hamburger Bahnhof, a complex exploration of the coexistence of living elements in a multisensory environment. This installation challenges conventional boundaries, urging viewers to immerse themselves in a dynamic, living ecosystem where the aroma of reindeer and the melodies of canaries contribute to a unique and thought-provoking artistic experience.
